The four ways to see a countdown without opening the app

  • Home Screen widget

    A small, medium or large tile on your Home Screen showing one or more countdowns at a glance.

  • Lock Screen widget

    Since iOS 16, widgets can sit right on the Lock Screen, so you see the countdown the moment you raise your phone.

  • StandBy widget

    When your iPhone is charging and turned sideways, StandBy mode shows a full-screen countdown — perfect as a nightstand clock.

  • Live Activity & Dynamic Island

    A real-time countdown that appears on the Lock Screen and in the Dynamic Island — great for the final hours before an event.

Frequently asked questions

Does iOS have a built-in countdown widget?

iOS does not include a dedicated countdown widget for arbitrary events out of the box. A countdown app like CNDWN adds Home Screen, Lock Screen and StandBy widgets that count down to any date you choose.

What is the difference between a widget and a Live Activity?

A widget sits on your Home Screen, Lock Screen or in StandBy and updates periodically. A Live Activity is a real-time, temporary display that appears on the Lock Screen and in the Dynamic Island while an event is actively approaching or in progress.

Can I put a countdown widget on my iPhone Lock Screen?

Yes. Since iOS 16, you can add widgets directly to the Lock Screen. Countdown apps like CNDWN support Lock Screen widgets that show the time remaining until your chosen date.
