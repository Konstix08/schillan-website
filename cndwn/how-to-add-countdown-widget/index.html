How to add a countdown widget on iPhone

Six steps, less than a minute — pin your first countdown to the Home Screen, Lock Screen or StandBy.

See CNDWN

Add your first countdown widget

You don't need any technical skill — just CNDWN installed and a date to count down to.

  • Download CNDWN

    Get CNDWN for free from the App Store and open it.

  • Create a countdown

    Tap +, add a title and pick a date — or import an event straight from Calendar or Reminders.

  • Long-press your Home Screen

    Touch and hold any empty area of your Home Screen until the icons start to jiggle.

  • Tap the + button

    Tap the + in the top corner to open the widget gallery.

  • Search for CNDWN

    Type "CNDWN" in the search bar and select it from the results.

  • Choose a size and add it

    Swipe to pick small, medium or large, then tap Add Widget. Tap Done to finish — your countdown now lives on your Home Screen.

Want it on your Lock Screen or StandBy instead?

The flow is nearly identical. For the Lock Screen, long-press it, tap Customize, choose the widget area below the clock and search for CNDWN. For StandBy, simply charge your iPhone and turn it sideways — StandBy activates automatically once you've added a compatible countdown widget. Frequently asked questions

Can I add more than one countdown widget?

Yes. You can add as many CNDWN widgets as you like, each showing a different countdown, and mix sizes across your Home Screen.

Do CNDWN widgets update automatically?

Yes. Once a widget is added, it keeps counting down on its own — there is no need to reopen the app to refresh it.

Are the steps the same on iPad?

Yes. The process is nearly identical on iPadOS: long-press the Home Screen, tap the + button and search for CNDWN in the widget gallery.
